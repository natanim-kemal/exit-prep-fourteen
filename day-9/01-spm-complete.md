# Software Project Management — Study Notes

## 1. Project Fundamentals

**Project**: temporary endeavor to create a unique product, service, or result. Has a defined start and end, unique deliverables, progressive elaboration.

**Project vs Program vs Portfolio**:
- **Project**: single, temporary, focused on specific deliverables
- **Program**: group of related projects managed together for coordinated benefits
- **Portfolio**: collection of projects, programs, and operations managed as a group to achieve strategic objectives

## 2. Project Planning

**Work Breakdown Structure (WBS)**: hierarchical decomposition of project work into manageable components. Foundation for planning, scheduling, budgeting.

**Scope management**: defines what is and isn't included in the project. Key tools: WBS, scope statement, requirements documentation.

## 3. Scheduling

**Gantt Chart**: bar chart showing tasks over time; visual representation of schedule, task durations, and dependencies.

**Critical Path Method (CPM)**: identifies the longest path of dependent tasks; determines minimum project duration. Tasks on critical path have zero float.

**PERT (Program Evaluation and Review Technique)**: uses optimistic, pessimistic, and most likely time estimates for probabilistic scheduling.

## 4. Cost Estimation

**Estimation techniques**:
- **Expert judgment**: based on experience
- **Analogous estimating**: using similar past projects
- **Parametric estimating**: using mathematical models (e.g., COCOMO)
- **Bottom-up estimating**: summing estimates of individual components
- **Three-point estimating**: optimistic + pessimistic + most likely

## 5. Risk Management

**Process**: Identification → Analysis (qualitative & quantitative) → Response Planning → Monitoring & Control.

**Risk response strategies**:
- **Avoidance**: eliminate the risk (change approach)
- **Mitigation**: reduce probability/impact
- **Transference**: transfer impact to third party (insurance, outsourcing)
- **Acceptance**: acknowledge and budget for impact

**Risk vs Issue**: Risk = potential problem (might happen); Issue = actual problem (has happened).

## 6. Quality Management

**Quality planning**: identify quality standards and how to meet them.
**Quality assurance (QA)**: process-oriented — ensures processes are followed.
**Quality control (QC)**: product-oriented — inspects deliverables.

## 7. Human Resource Management

- **Organizational planning**: identify roles, responsibilities, reporting relationships
- **Staff acquisition**: hire/bring in team members
- **Team development**: training, team-building activities

**Project manager responsibilities**: planning, organizing, staffing, directing, controlling, communicating.

## 8. Communication Management

- **Stakeholder analysis**: identify all stakeholders and their information needs
- **Communication plan**: who needs what information, when, how

## 9. Monitoring & Control

**Earned Value Management (EVM)**: integrates scope, schedule, and cost:
- **PV (Planned Value)**: budgeted cost of work scheduled
- **EV (Earned Value)**: budgeted cost of work performed
- **AC (Actual Cost)**: actual cost of work performed
- **CPI = EV/AC**: cost efficiency ( >1 = under budget)
- **SPI = EV/PV**: schedule efficiency ( >1 = ahead of schedule)
